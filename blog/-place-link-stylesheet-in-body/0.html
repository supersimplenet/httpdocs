Cookie policy

  1. 1. What are cookies?

    Cookies (or, cookies) are files that store and retrieve information about the user's browsing habits. The information obtained concerns, for example, the number of pages viewed, the language, the place from which the user accesses the site, the number of new users, the frequency and repetition of the consultations, their duration, the browser or the computer on which the consultation is carried out, or on which the application is executed.

  2. 2. What types of cookies does AMCORE BALANCE use on its website?

    The typology of cookies used when browsing the website is established as follows:

    Depending on the entity that operates them:

    • Own cookies: they are sent to the user's terminal device, from a computer or a domain managed by their own publisher, and from which the service requested by the user is carried out.
    • Third party cookies: they are sent to the user's terminal device, from a computer or domain whose management is not the responsibility of the publisher, but of another entity which processes the data obtained by means of cookies.

    According to their period of activity:

    • Session cookies: they collect and store data when the user accesses the website.
    • Persistent cookies: they collect data stored on the terminal, and are subject to access and processing for a given period.

    According to their purpose:

    • Technical cookies: they allow navigation on the website and the use of the various services found there, for example, to control traffic and communication of data, carry out the procedure for purchasing an order, or use elements of safety during navigation.
    • Personalization cookies: they allow the user to access the service according to predefined characteristics.
    • Analysis cookies: they allow monitoring and analysis of user behavior (for example, to measure application activity, develop user profiles, etc. in order to make improvements to the service provided, according to the data relating to the use made by users).
    • Advertising cookies: they allow the effective management of the advertising space included on the website, based on various criteria such as the content published or the frequency of exposure to the ads.
    • Behavioral advertising cookies: they allow the most efficient management of the advertising space included on the website. These cookies store information about user behavior, obtained through continuous observation of their browsing habits; a specific profile can thus be developed in order to display the advertisements that correspond to it.
